Journalism for change

projects

At the heart of our projects is great content that builds communities and has lasting impact

Launched in 2013, Iranwire is a project supported by IREX, to support independent journalism and journalists in Iran. Iranwire has extensive coverage of news, sport and current affairs as well as reporting from the provinces. This project consists of a web publishing platform in Persian and English. We also have a Persian Facebook page as well as an English Facebook page and a Persian Twitter feed as well as an English Twitter feed.

Currently being relaunched, Journalism Is Not A Crime is an umbrella project to bring attention to the treatment of individual journalists as well as the necessity of free speech, debate and expression. The project includes original research, several documentary films, a campaigning web platform, individual support for persecuted journalists and two books.

Education Is Not A Crime is a global campaign to fight the barring of Iranian Baha’is from the country’s universities. Inspired by Maziar Bahari’s 2014 documentary film To Light a Candle, the campaign quickly won support from Nobel Peace Prize laureates Archbishop Desmond Tutu, Dr Shirin Ebadi, and three others, and several other prominent figures.

Freedom beat is a web platform and private network of musicians from across the Middle-East, many of whom are banned from performing in public and have had their work censored. This project showcases their work and is creating and mentoring a community of like-minded individuals across the region

This is our blog where we cover the work of independent journalists from around the world and build relationships with them. From data journalism in Greece and India, to radio in El Salvador, this is where we learn about some of the most exciting media projects out there.

Burundi Voices brings the power of live drumming performances to a population riven by memories of genocide. The 'safe space' of the drumming circle allows heart-rending testimonies to be heard both by the victims and perpetrators paving the way for reconciliation and healing.

OUR TOOLS

We produce across as many different media as possible to entertain and engage with the widest possible audience.

We produce a huge range of factual and impartial reporting. We report on the full range of news stories including politics, finance and economics, sport, culture, conflict, social issues and human interest. We commission and support independent journalists from around the world as well as citizen-journalists and bloggers. We are able to source storires from some of the most inaccessible areas including the provinces of Iran and the tribal areas of Afghanistan. Our blog covers some of the best independent journalism in the world including online and new media projects, print, video journalism and radio.

Our documentaries, both long-form and short-form, are professionally shot and directed to the highest standards. To Light A Candle an hour-long documentary, partly filmed covertly in Iran that uncovers the discrimination faced by members of Iran's Baha'i religious minority who are denied entry to Iran's universities. My Return To The Valley Of Death follows a young Pashtun exile as he journeys home. Filmed in the tribal areas of Afghanistan, this is a unique, insiders view of one of the most dangerous conflict zones in the world. Journalism For Change has also produced filmed interviews with nominees of the Rory `Peck Awards in independent journalism and has also made films about journalists in India, Serbia, Burma, Turkey and Greece.

Our web-producers have created the campaigning platforms Journalism Is Not A Crime and Education Is Not A Crime. With a wide range of graphics and video content as well as the latest in html and css user-experience creation, our platforms are designed to be accessible in a wide range of countries. We also make sure that our content can be read across a range of desktop and handheld devices.
